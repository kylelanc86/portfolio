About Me

Background

Hello, my name is Kyle. I am relatively new to the world of programming having formally starting my education in the field in 2023. In the past I ran my own environmental consultancy which was a very educational and enjoyable experience. After 15 years in that field, I decided I wanted a change. In early 2024 my partner gave brith to my daughter, Maeve, and I decided i wanted a career which offered me more dlexibility in terms of hours, working from home etc. rather than a site-based job where my time was dictated by client requirements.

Over the past 12 months I have learnt much about HTML, CSS and Javascript and have utilised my new skills to develop a variety of webistes, both client-paid projects and projects I have taken on simply to improve my skillset. This webpage details some of the websites I have developed along with details on techniques I have utilised.


Study & Development

CS50

From January 2024 I began following the Harvard CS50 programming course which gave an overarching understanding in programming and problem solving. Whilst I enjoyed this course and found it interesting, I struggled with what I felt as a chasm between the taught content and the requirements for the problem sets. The course did a good job of reinforcing my interest, but after completing the 8th week of the course I decided to abandon the course. I consider myself a good problem solver, however because at this point in my learning journey I still have a lot to learn, I think that I will enjpy the course and get more out of the problem solving elements once I have a better skillset.


CodeAcademy

From May 2024, after a bit of research on different educational tools, I decided to sign up for CodeAcademy. As I am working from home and dealing with a young infant it was important for me that I followed a program which was easy to follow and pick up for even small periods of time. I also enjopyed the concept that you could follow a 'syllabus' which is orientated towards a specific role.As of August 2024 I am half way through a full-stack engineer syllabus which I am hoping will give me a broad understanding of the full spectrum of roles open to a programmer before I focus on a specific pathway.
